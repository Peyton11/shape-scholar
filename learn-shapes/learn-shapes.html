3-Dimensional Shapes

  • Cube- A three-dimensional shape with six equal square faces, twelve edges, and eight vertices. It's a type of rectangular prism where all sides are equal.
  • Sphere- A perfectly round 3D object, like a ball, with every point on its surface equidistant from its center. It has no edges or vertices.
  • Cylinder- A solid with two parallel circular bases connected by a curved surface at a fixed distance from the center of the circles.
  • Cone- A shape with a circular base that tapers smoothly to a single point called the apex.
  • Pyramid- A polyhedron with a polygon base and triangular faces that meet at a single point, called the apex.
  • Triangular Prism- A prism with triangular bases and three rectangular faces connecting the corresponding sides of the triangles.
  • Rectangular Prism- A prism with six rectangular faces, twelve edges, and eight vertices, with opposite faces being equal and parallel.
  • Pentagonal Prism- A prism with two pentagonal bases and five rectangular sides connecting the bases.
  • Hexagonal Prism- A prism with two hexagonal bases and six rectangular sides connecting the bases.
  • Heptagonal Prism- A prism with two heptagonal bases (seven-sided polygons) and seven rectangular sides.
  • Octagonal Prism- A prism with two octagonal bases (eight-sided polygons) and eight rectangular sides.
  • Nonagonal Prism- A prism with two nonagonal bases (nine-sided polygons) and nine rectangular sides.
  • Ellipsoid- A 3D shape resembling a stretched sphere, where the radii along three perpendicular axes can differ.
  • Torus- A donut-shaped 3D object formed by revolving a circle around an axis outside of itself.
  • Octahedron- A polyhedron with eight equilateral triangular faces, twelve edges, and six vertices.
  • Tetrahedron A polyhedron with four triangular faces, six edges, and four vertices. It's the simplest form of a pyramid.
  • Dodecahedron- A polyhedron with twelve pentagonal faces, twenty vertices, and thirty edges.
  • Icosahedron- A polyhedron with twenty equilateral triangular faces, twelve vertices, and thirty edges.
  • Frustrum- A truncated cone or pyramid, formed by cutting the shape parallel to its base and removing the top portion.
  • Hexagonal Antiprism- A polyhedron with two parallel hexagonal bases connected by an alternating band of triangular faces.
  • Star- A star-shaped 3D object often represented as a stellated polyhedron, formed by extending the faces or edges of a simpler polyhedron.
  • Heart- A heart-shaped 3D object typically created by combining curves like circles and parabolas.
